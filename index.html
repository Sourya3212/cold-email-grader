How this score is calculated
Every email starts at 100. Points come off for signals that real spam filters and mailbox providers react to. Nothing here is a guess about the words you meant, it is about how the message reads to a filter.
  • Spam-trigger words and hype phrases, weighted by how strong the signal is
  • Unfilled mail-merge tags, a dead giveaway of a broken mass send
  • Lookalike (homoglyph) characters used to sneak past filters
  • Link shorteners, link overload, and image-only or hidden-text HTML
  • ALL CAPS, exclamation pile-ups, and money or discount symbols
  • A missing opt-out, an impersonal greeting, and subject-line red flags
This is a content and formatting check. It does not test your domain authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) or your sending reputation, which are the other half of whether mail lands. Want those checked too? Details are in the footer.
